## Case Study: Cyberbullying 

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through social media, messaging platforms, or other digital spaces with harmful, abusive, or threatening content. It often includes spreading rumors, posting offensive comments, sharing private information, or repeatedly targeting someone to cause emotional distress.

A typical cyberbullying incident begins when a student posts content online. The bully, often someone known to the victim, starts by making negative or sarcastic comments publicly. Gradually, the behavior escalates to direct messages, spreading false rumors, or sharing edited images to embarrass the victim. In many cases, fake accounts are created to continue harassment anonymously, making it difficult to trace the offender.

College students and teenagers are the most common targets due to their active presence on social media and peer influence. Victims often experience anxiety, low self-esteem, academic decline, and in severe cases, depression. The public nature of online platforms amplifies the harm, as content can spread rapidly and remain accessible for long periods.

For instance, a realistic scenario involves a college student being repeatedly targeted through memes and abusive comments on social media by peers. Despite blocking the accounts, new fake profiles continue the harassment, leading to emotional distress and withdrawal from social activities.

This case highlights the importance of digital responsibility, reporting abusive behavior, and promoting a safe and respectful online environment.
